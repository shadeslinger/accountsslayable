## 1. What This Business Is

Accounts Slayable is a digital financial planning brand selling Google Sheets templates, educational content, and eventually services — targeting freelancers, consultants, young professionals, and first-time homebuyers. The core differentiator is an authentic, comedic, queer-friendly voice that makes personal finance feel approachable rather than intimidating.

The brand sits at the intersection of **practical financial tools + identity-affirming tone + educational content**. Most finance templates feel like a beige office. Accounts Slayable feels safe, funny, and real.

---

## 2. Strategic Evolution (How We Got Here)

The strategy has evolved through three phases:

**Phase 1 — Template Business Model (Original)**  
Broad Google Sheets template suite (15-20 templates) sold across Etsy, Gumroad, and a Shopify site. Targeting young professionals and first-time homebuyers. Budget: $3,000-$15,000 startup. Revenue target: $25,000-$80,000 year one.

**Phase 2 — Live Streaming + AI Content Multiplication**  
Pivoted to streaming-first approach (2x weekly) with AI tools (OpusClip, Descript, Restream) automatically repurposing each stream into clips across TikTok, Instagram, Snapchat, and YouTube. Recognized that authentic community building through live content is more valuable than a template-only approach.

**Phase 3 — Wedge Strategy + Performance Funnels (Current)**  
Narrowed from broad template suite to a focused "one-problem wedge" launch. Instead of shipping 15 templates at once, lead with ONE high-intent bundle that solves a specific, painful problem. Build a measurable funnel from day one. Layer in services, matching, and community over time.

**Key Insight:** Each phase has been smarter about constraints — $1,500 budget, ~10 hours/week, solo operator. The current strategy is the most realistic and scalable given those constraints.

## 3. Target Audiences (Prioritized)

### Primary: Independent Consultants & Creators (1099 Workers)
- **Why first:** Aligned with Adam's voice and likely streaming audience. High pain (quarterly taxes, messy income tracking, no employer benefits). Willing to pay for solutions. Creates natural upsell paths.
- **Pain points:** Irregular income tracking, quarterly tax set-aside confusion, receipt chaos, invoice management, "am I even profitable?" anxiety.

### Secondary: Young Professionals & First-Time Homebuyers
- **Why second:** Large addressable market. Strong SEO potential. Aligns with existing market research and competitor analysis.
- **Pain points:** Paycheck-to-paycheck budgeting, student debt management, down payment savings tracking, mortgage readiness confusion.

### Tertiary (Future): Small Nonprofits & Grant-Funded Orgs
- **Why later:** High-value niche with compliance-driven urgency, but different audience from initial community. Launch only if demand signals appear.
- **Pain points:** Grant budget tracking, allowable cost compliance, audit readiness, burn rate reporting.

## 4. Brand Positioning

### Voice & Tone
- Playful, comedic, and authentic — not corporate or "finance bro"
- Queer-friendly and identity-affirming as brand texture (not necessarily lead positioning — Adam to decide)
- Makes complex financial concepts feel manageable and even fun
- "Serious financial tools with a not-so-serious vibe"

### Brand Identity
- **Primary Color:** Sage green (#87A96B) — growth and money
- **Secondary Color:** Warm cream (#F7F5F3) — simplicity and clarity
- **Accent Color:** Coral pink (#FF6B6B) — energy and approachability
- **Typography:** Montserrat (headlines), Open Sans (body)
- **Tagline options:** "Your Financial Future, Simplified" / "Slay your accounts, not your sanity"

### Key Differentiators vs. Competitors
1. **Voice moat** — Most templates/apps are clinical or generic. Accounts Slayable has personality.
2. **Education baked in** — Not just a spreadsheet, but context, tips, and "when to ask a pro" guidance in every template.
3. **Wedge specificity** — Solving ONE problem deeply (1099 money management) rather than everything shallowly.
4. **One-time pricing** — No subscription fatigue. Templates are buy-once.
5. **Community-driven** — Live streams, office hours, "ask without shame" culture.

## 6. Content & Marketing Strategy

### Content Pillars (with humor baked in)
1. **Receipts & Reality** — "Here's what this number actually means"
2. **Money Boundaries** — Pricing, late payers, scope creep, saying no
3. **Grant Gremlins** — Compliance, reporting, budget narratives (when audience demands it)
4. **Spreadsheet Glow-Ups** — Before/after transformations, template walkthroughs

### Content Engine: Live Streaming → AI Repurposing
- **2x weekly live streams** (primary content creation)
- **AI repurposing pipeline:** OpusClip, Descript, Restream
- Each stream → 5-10 short clips across TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, Snapchat
- Clips drive traffic to lead magnets and template sales

### Weekly Cadence
- **1 newsletter** — Story + lesson + CTA (template, quiz, or consult)
- **2 short social posts** — Hot take + practical tip
- **1 "tool drop"** — Template snippet, mini calculator, or spreadsheet trick
- **2 live streams** — Educational/entertaining, building community

### SEO & Blog Strategy
- Target long-tail keywords: "1099 quarterly tax calculator template," "freelancer budget Google Sheets," "first-time homebuyer budget template 2026"
- Each blog post naturally showcases a template with screenshots and CTA
- Prioritize U.S.-specific content (U.S. tax rules, student loans, housing market)

### Email Marketing
- **Welcome sequence (5 emails / 7 days):** Lead magnet delivery → success story → common mistakes → advanced tip → product pitch
- **Weekly newsletter:** Financial tip + template spotlight + community highlight
- **Segmentation via quiz:** Route freelancers vs. homebuyers vs. general audience to tailored sequences

### Social Platforms (Priority Order)
1. **TikTok** — Primary discovery engine. 1-2 posts daily. 40% educational, 30% entertaining, 20% inspirational, 10% promotional.
2. **Instagram** — Reels + Stories for community building. Cross-post TikTok content + behind-the-scenes.
3. **YouTube** — Long-form tutorials, stream archives, template walkthroughs. SEO value.
4. **Pinterest** — Passive SEO play for template discovery. Design pins linking to blog posts and products.

## 7. Sales & Distribution

### Platform Strategy

| Platform | Role | Fees | Priority |
|----------|------|------|----------|
| **Own Website** | Brand hub, direct sales, blog, email capture | Hosting + payment processing (~3%) | Primary |
| **Etsy** | Marketplace discovery, search-driven sales | ~10% total (listing + transaction + processing) | Secondary |
| **Gumroad** | Social media checkout, affiliate program | 10% per sale, no monthly fee | Tertiary |

### Pricing Principles
- One-time purchase model (no subscriptions for core templates)
- Bundle discounts: 20-25% off when buying related templates together
- Introductory launch pricing (40-50% off first week)
- Messaging: "Get your entire financial planner for $29 — less than one month of a budgeting app subscription"
- Value anchoring against competitor subscription costs (YNAB $99/yr, Monarch $100/yr)

## 12. Competitive Landscape Summary

### Direct Competitors

| Competitor | Model | Price | Weakness for Us to Exploit |
|-----------|-------|-------|----------------------------|
| Etsy template sellers | One-off sheets, low price | $5-$15 | No education, no community, no personality |
| YNAB | Subscription app | $99/yr | Expensive, no customization, no 1099 focus |
| Tiller Money | Auto-sync spreadsheets | $79/yr | Subscription model, not targeted to freelancers |
| Debt Free Mom | Template + mini-course | $9 | Narrow focus (paycheck budgeting), different audience |
| Rocket Money | Fintech app | $6-$12/mo | Automation-focused, no DIY/education angle |

### Our Strategic Advantage
The market is bifurcated between **expensive automated apps** and **cheap generic templates**. Accounts Slayable occupies the middle: affordable one-time purchases with personality, education, and community that generic templates lack, without the subscription cost of apps.
